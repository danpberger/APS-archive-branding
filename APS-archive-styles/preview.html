Learning dynamics of complex systems from partial observations

March 6, 2023, 11:00 AM – 11:12 AM

Abstract

Dynamical systems are used throughout the sciences to model complex real-world phenomena. However, building accurate models from data remains challenging, especially when only partial observations of the system are available. We develop an automated inference method that discovers predictive differential equation models from a few noisy partial observations. Our approach combines sparse regression with neural network embeddings to identify governing equations even when key variables are unmeasured. We demonstrate our method on biological neural networks, gene regulatory networks, and fluid dynamics systems, showing that accurate models can be learned from incomplete data. This work opens new possibilities for scientific discovery in systems where comprehensive measurements are impractical. The mathematical framework involves coupled ODEs of the form dx/dt = f(x, y) where x represents observed variables and y represents hidden states. We show that under certain conditions on the observability of the system, the hidden dynamics can be reconstructed up to a diffeomorphism.
